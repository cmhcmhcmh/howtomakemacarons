The History of the French Macaron

  • Not to be confused with a "macaroon," a "macaron" is light and airy with almond, sugar, and an egg whites-based shell. The "macaroon" is a baked confection of egg whites, sugar, and shredded dried coconut.



  • Born in Italy, introduced by the chef of Catherine de Medicis in 1533 at her time of marriage
  • The name "macaron" has the same origin as the word "macaroni," both meaning "fine dough"
  • Macarons are the most coveted cookie in France, particularly Paris.
  • Trendy item for people to serve
  • Ideal breakfast treat, beloved cookie of Parisian tea salons, fashionable gift, ideal cookie for Valentine's Day and Mother's Day
  • Macarons are not as successful in the United States than they are in Europe and France, and though they can be found in the states the quality is not always as high.
